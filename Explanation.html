Explanation

1. Answer: C
Explanation: The Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER) is a continuous system of membranous tubules and sacs. The Rough ER is specifically continuous with the outer membrane of the nuclear envelope, acting as a pipeline for molecules between the nucleus and the cytoplasm.
2. Answer: A
Explanation: In the Fluid Mosaic Model, "fluidity" refers to the ability of lipid molecules to move. Phospholipids are not fixed; they rotate and move laterally within their layer, allowing the membrane to be flexible and self-repairing.
3. Answer: C
Explanation: The nucleolus is a non-membrane bound structure within the nucleus where ribosomal RNA (rRNA) is synthesized and combined with proteins to form ribosomal subunits. Without it, the cell cannot produce ribosomes.
4. Answer: C
Explanation: Secretory proteins are synthesized by ribosomes on the RER, transported to the Golgi for modification and packaging into secretory vesicles, which then fuse with the plasma membrane (exocytosis).
5. Answer: B
Explanation: The Smooth ER (SER) contains enzymes that catalyze reactions to make drugs, alcohol, and metabolic byproducts more water-soluble, allowing the body to flush them out.
6. Answer: C
Explanation: Microtubules are hollow cylinders roughly 25nm in diameter, built from dimers of alpha-tubulin and beta-tubulin proteins.
7. Answer: A
Explanation: Lysosomes contain about 40 different types of digestive (hydrolytic) enzymes. Autophagy occurs when lysosomes fuse with damaged organelles to digest them and recycle their components.
8. Answer: C
Explanation: The primary cell wall is a thin, flexible layer formed while the cell is growing. It is composed primarily of cellulose microfibrils embedded in a matrix of pectin and hemicellulose.
9. Answer: B
Explanation: The inner mitochondrial membrane is highly convoluted into folds called cristae. This maximizes the surface area available for the enzymes of the Electron Transport Chain and ATP Synthase (F1 particles).
10. Answer: C
Explanation: According to the endosymbiotic theory, chloroplasts and mitochondria were once independent prokaryotes. Hence, they still possess their own circular DNA and 70S ribosomes (unlike the 80S ribosomes in the rest of the eukaryotic cell).
11. Answer: C
Explanation: The Golgi apparatus is polarized. The cis face (forming face) is located near the ER to receive transport vesicles, while the trans face (maturing face) acts as the shipping side.
12. Answer: A
Explanation: A centriole is a cylindrical structure made of nine triplets of microtubules arranged in a ring (9+0 pattern). Since there are 2 centrioles in a centrosome, they are perpendicular to each other.
13. Answer: C
Explanation: Glyoxysomes are specialized peroxisomes found in plants. They contain enzymes for the glyoxylate cycle, which converts stored lipids (fatty acids) into carbohydrates (succinate/sugar) to provide energy for a germinating seedling.
14. Answer: C
Explanation: Nuclear pores are selective. DNA and chromatin stay inside the nucleus, but mRNA and assembled ribosomal subunits must exit to the cytoplasm for protein synthesis.
15. Answer: D
Explanation: The three main components of the cytoskeleton are microtubules, microfilaments (actin), and intermediate filaments. Myofilaments (actin and myosin) are the specialized contractile machinery of muscle cells, not the general structural cytoskeleton of all cells.
16. Answer: C
Explanation: Al2(SO4)3 contains 4 × 3 = 12 oxygen atoms per formula unit. Moles of O = 0.5 mol of compound × 12 O atoms = 6 moles.
17. Answer: B
Explanation: Moles = Mass / Molar Mass. 16g O2 = 16/32 = 0.5 mol. 14g N2 = 14/28 = 0.5 mol. According to Avogadro’s law, equal moles contain an equal number of molecules.
18. Answer: A
Explanation: Moles of N2 = 2.8 / 28 = 0.1 mol. Volume = Moles × 22.414 dm³ = 0.1 × 22.4 = 2.24 dm³.
19. Answer: B
Explanation: C = 40/12 = 3.33; H = 6.7/1 = 6.7; O = 53.3/16 = 3.33. Divide by the smallest value (3.33) → C=1, H=2, O=1. Empirical Formula: CH2O.
20. Answer: D
Explanation: Isotopes have the same atomic number (protons) and electronic configuration, meaning they have identical chemical properties. However, their different masses result in different physical properties, such as the rate of diffusion (Grahams's Law).
21. Answer: B
Explanation: Reaction: CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O. 16g CH4 (1 mol) produces 36g H2O (2 moles). Therefore, 1.6g CH4 (0.1 mol) produces 3.6g H2O.
22. Answer: B
Explanation: Molar mass of NaCl = 58.5 g/mol. Moles = 5.85 / 58.5 = 0.1 mol. Formula units = 0.1 × 6.02 × 1023 = 6.02 × 1022.
23. Answer: C
Explanation: The International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC) adopted Carbon-12 as the standard in 1961, replacing Oxygen.
24. Answer: C
Explanation: The limiting reactant is the substance that is totally consumed when the chemical reaction is complete. The amount of product formed is limited by this reagent because the reaction cannot continue without it.
25. Answer: B
Explanation: Mass of one atom = Atomic mass / NA. 12 / (6 × 1023) = 2 × 10-23 g.
26. Answer: B
Explanation: Urea (NH2)2CO has a molar mass of 60 g/mol. It contains 2 Nitrogen atoms (28g). %N = (28/60) × 100 = 46.6%.
27. Answer: B
Explanation: H2SO4 has 2 moles of H atoms. Mass = 2 moles × 1.008 g/mol = 2.016 g.
28. Answer: D
Explanation: In stoichiometry, we assume the law of conservation of mass holds perfectly, meaning no product is lost, and we assume the reactants are pure so that the calculated mass ratios are accurate.
29. Answer: B
Explanation: 22.4 L = 1 mole. 11.2 L = 0.5 moles. Mass of CH4 = 0.5 mol × 16 g/mol = 8 g.
30. Answer: C
Explanation: A mass spectrometer separates isotopes based on their mass-to-charge ratio (m/e). The detector records the intensity of each ion stream, which corresponds to the relative abundance of that isotope.
31. Answer: D
Explanation: The maximum resultant is 3+4=7. The minimum is 4-3=1. Any value outside the range [1, 7] is impossible. 8 is outside this range.
32. Answer: A
Explanation: |A × B| = AB sin θ. For parallel vectors, θ = 0° and sin 0 = 0. The result of a cross product is always a vector, so the result is a Null Vector.
33. Answer: B
Explanation: The first condition for equilibrium states that for a body to have no linear acceleration (translational equilibrium), the vector sum of all external forces must be zero (Σ F = 0).
34. Answer: B
Explanation: A · B = AB cos θ and |A × B| = AB sin θ. If AB cos θ = AB sin θ ⇒ tan θ = 1 ⇒ θ = 45°.
35. Answer: A
Explanation: A unit vector â is defined as the vector A divided by its magnitude |A|. It indicates direction only and has a magnitude of 1.
36. Answer: B
Explanation: Fy = F sin θ = 10 sin 60°. Since sin 60° = √3/2, then Fy = 10 × (√3/2) = 5√3 N.
37. Answer: C
Explanation: Torque is the cross product τ = r × F. The direction of the resulting vector is determined by the Right Hand Rule (curl fingers from r to F, thumb points to τ).
38. Answer: C
Explanation: A couple is formed by two parallel forces that are equal in magnitude, opposite in direction, and do not share the same line of action. It produces pure rotation (torque) but no translation.
39. Answer: B
Explanation: Ax = A cos θ and Ay = A sin θ. They are equal when cos θ = sin θ, which occurs at 45°.
40. Answer: B
Explanation: The dot product is A · B = AB cos θ. Since î (x-axis) and ĵ (y-axis) are perpendicular, θ = 90° and cos 90° = 0.
41. Answer: C
Explanation: For "Complete Equilibrium," a body must satisfy both the first condition (Σ F = 0, no linear acceleration) and the second condition (Σ τ = 0, no angular acceleration).
42. Answer: B
Explanation: Rectangular components are the projections of a vector along the axes of a Cartesian coordinate system, which are by definition mutually perpendicular to one another.
43. Answer: B
Explanation: (Same logic as Q34). When the magnitudes are equal, sin θ = cos θ, which happens at 45°.
44. Answer: B
Explanation: τ = rF sin θ. Torque is maximum when sin θ is maximum. sin θ = 1 when θ = 90°.
45. Answer: A
Explanation: The position vector r of a point (x, y) is written as xî + yĵ. For (3, 4), it is 3î + 4ĵ.
46. Answer: C
Explanation: When two actions happened in the past, the action that was completed first uses the Past Perfect (had + V3). The building burned before the firemen arrived.
47. Answer: C
Explanation: The word "yet" is a time marker for the Present Perfect tense, indicating an action that was expected to happen but hasn't happened up to the present moment.
48. Answer: C
Explanation: "Since 2010" indicates a point in time. For an action that started in the past and is still continuing, the Present Perfect Continuous (has/have + been + V-ing) is the most appropriate.
49. Answer: A
Explanation: This is a Type 1 (First) Conditional. The "if-clause" uses the Simple Present (rains), and the "main clause" uses the Future Simple (will cancel).
50. Answer: C
Explanation: Universal truths, habits, and scientific facts are always expressed in the Simple Present tense.
