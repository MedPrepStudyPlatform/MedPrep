Which of the following correctly describes the mechanism of action of N-acetylcysteine in the treatment of acetaminophen (paracetamol) overdose? A 22-year-old woman is brought to the emergency department 6 hours after ingesting 25 grams of acetaminophen with suicidal intent. She is started on intravenous N-acetylcysteine.
A. Directly binds to acetaminophen in the gut, preventing absorption and enterohepatic recirculation
B. Alkalinizes the urine, trapping acetaminophen metabolites and enhancing renal excretion
C. Induces CYP2E1, accelerating metabolism of acetaminophen to non-toxic metabolites
D. Replenishes glutathione stores and directly neutralizes the reactive metabolite NAPQI
E. Competes with acetaminophen for protein binding, reducing free drug concentration in the blood
Explanation
Acetaminophen is metabolized by CYP2E1 to a toxic intermediate, NAPQI, which is normally detoxified by glutathione. N-acetylcysteine replenishes glutathione stores and directly neutralizes NAPQI. It is most effective when given within 8-10 hours of ingestion. The FDA-approved protocol uses a 21-hour intravenous infusion.
